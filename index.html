Why these moves

  • Morning stiffness responds best to slow, gentle mobility that warms tissues before deeper stretching, which reduces discomfort and supports daily movement quality [16][8].
  • Hip flexor and hamstring work helps offload the low back, especially after prolonged sitting or sleeping, while spinal flows restore segmental motion safely [5][7].
  • Chest and neck openers counter forward‑head and rounded‑shoulder posture common in students and desk work, improving comfort through the day [6][5].

Tips and safety

  • Warm up gradually, breathe steadily, and stop at mild tension; avoid bouncing and any painful end‑range positions [16].
  • Keep knees soft in forward folds and hinge at the hips; use blocks or thighs for support if hamstrings are tight [5][7].
  • If floors are not ideal, many moves can start in bed or standing (cobra/mini backbend in bed, knees‑to‑chest, side bend, neck stretch) to ease into the day [6].

Optional bed or desk variations

In bed

  • Gentle cobra, single‑knee‑to‑chest, and supine spinal twist to wake up the spine without loading, 20–30 seconds each [6].

At a desk

  • Seated upper‑back reach (hands interlaced, arms forward), cross‑body shoulder stretch, and doorway chest stretch between classes, 20–30 seconds each [6][5].

10‑minute add‑ons

  • Add Thread‑the‑Needle from tabletop for mid‑back/shoulder relief, ~30–45 seconds per side [7].
  • Add standing quad stretch, ~30 seconds per side to balance hip mobility [6].
  • Add doorway chest stretch or hands‑clasped chest opener, ~30 seconds to counter rounded shoulders [5][6].
  • Add wall calf stretch, ~30 seconds per side to ease morning tightness [6].
Breathe slow, smooth, pain‑free. Hold mild tension only. Avoid bouncing [16][5]. Use at your own pace.
